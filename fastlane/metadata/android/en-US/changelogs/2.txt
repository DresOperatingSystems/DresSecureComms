1.5.0

Security hardening release.

- App Lock now binds to a hardware Keystore key: unlocking decrypts a stored marker, so it
  cannot be bypassed by a spoofed callback or hooking tools. Falls back to the device PIN or
  pattern on phones without a strong biometric.
- New encrypted messages use PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 (AES-256-GCM). Messages you already received
  stay readable; the other person should also update to exchange new encrypted messages.
- Locked screen no longer lays out content and keeps screenshot protection.
- Added tapjacking protection on sensitive screens.
- Network access is restricted to HTTPS for the VirusTotal scan.
- Release builds carry a proper signing certificate and omit the Google dependency-metadata blob.
