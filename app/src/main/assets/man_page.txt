DresSecureComms is a FOSS, de-Googled, all-in-one secure communications app.

Features:
- Messages: a private, offline SMS client. Send and receive normal texts, or flip on
  encryption per message. Encrypted messages are AES-256-GCM and only work between two
  people who both use this app and share the same key (set by hand in Settings), also comes with photo sharing and group creation.
- Calls: dial any number or call a saved contact. Calls use your normal carrier line.
- Contacts: an encrypted local vault. Add contacts by hand or import them from the device.
  Everything is stored encrypted at rest using the Android Keystore.
- Threat Scan: check a URL against VirusTotal using your own free API key.
- Metadata Wipe: strip GPS and EXIF metadata from a photo before you share it.
- Geo Spoofer: set a mock GPS location, or have the app pick a random one. Enable this app
  as the mock location app under Developer options first.
- App Lock: biometric or device PIN protection.
- Block Screenshots: stop screenshots and screen recording inside the app.

Allowing SMS (one-time, required):
Because this app is installed from outside the Play Store, Android may block its SMS access
under "Restricted settings". If SMS does not work, do this once:
  1. Open Android Settings, then Apps, then DresSecureComms.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu in the top-right corner.
  3. Tap "Allow restricted settings".
  4. Reopen DresSecureComms and grant the SMS permission when asked.

How it works:
- SMS and calls work fully offline on your carrier line. No Google services, no trackers.
- Internet is only used by Threat Scan.
- For encrypted SMS, share the key with your contact by hand. Only this app can read it.

DresSecureComms is a core part of DresOS, the Android defensive security system. It brings
private messaging, calling, contacts, threat scanning, and location protection together in
one place so much of the system's day-to-day protection runs through this single app. You
can read more about the wider project from the About screen.
